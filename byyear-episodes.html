Yearly gender split on UK comedy panel shows

By @astronomyblog. Last updated 20 March 2017

A heat-map showing a yearly breakdown of gender splits across episodes of UK comedy panel shows on TV and radio. The gender split for every comedy panel show episode is calculated and then binned to make a heat map. The darker the green colour of a cell, the more episodes have that split. A minimum shading has been applied as some bins were too faint to see. Individual episodes should vary so we expect a distribution of gender splits. If things were equal, you would expect the distribution to peak around the middle of each row. The banding is due to the fact that panels are often made of 5 or 7 individuals.

A summary of 4884 episodes since 1967 (4 episodes with no appearance data):

  • 0.37% (18 episodes) are at least two-thirds female (1.25% in 2016);
  • 4.44% (217 episodes) are equal or majority female (9.38% in 2016);
  • 0.04% (2 episodes) are all female (0.00% in 2016).
  • 30.55% (1492 episodes) are all male (2.81% in 2016);

Note that there are some episodes (particularly older ones) where we don't have the complete line-up as these were not included in the sources (you can find these on the individual show episode-by-episode breakdowns). If anyone has evidence of unlisted people in specific episodes, please get in touch. As a result, some of the precise numbers above are subject to a slight uncertainty. Specifically, there may be a small percentage of episodes which are counted as 'all male' which may have had a female guest who was omitted in the source e.g. The Radio Times, iPlayer etc. The 'all female' episode number is not affected in the same way.
